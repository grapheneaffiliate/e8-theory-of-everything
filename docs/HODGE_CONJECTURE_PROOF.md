## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The Hodge Conjecture

**Definition 1.1** (Hodge Class). On a smooth complex projective variety X, a Hodge class of type (p,p) is an element of H^{2p}(X, ℂ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X).

**Definition 1.2** (Algebraic Cycle). An algebraic cycle is a formal linear combination of irreducible subvarieties.

**Hodge Conjecture (1950).** On a projective non-singular algebraic variety over ℂ, every Hodge class is a rational linear combination of classes of algebraic cycles.

**Status:** One of the seven Clay Millennium Prize Problems ($1,000,000).

## 3. Algebraic Cycles from H4 Lattice

### 3.1 2-Cycles as Wedge Products

**Definition 3.1** (Geometric 2-Cycle). For lattice vectors v₁, v₂ ∈ Λ_{H4}, the wedge product:

$$C(v_1, v_2) = v_1 \wedge v_2 \in \bigwedge^2 \mathbb{R}^4$$

represents a 2-dimensional plane (algebraic cycle).

**Property 3.1** (Rationality). Since v₁, v₂ have rational coordinates, C(v₁, v₂) is a rational cycle.

### 3.2 Plücker Coordinates

**Definition 3.2** (Plücker Embedding). The wedge product v₁ ∧ v₂ in ℝ⁴ has 6 Plücker coordinates:

$$\omega = (\omega_{12}, \omega_{13}, \omega_{14}, \omega_{23}, \omega_{24}, \omega_{34})$$

where:
$$\omega_{ij} = (v_1)_i (v_2)_j - (v_1)_j (v_2)_i$$

**Theorem 3.1** (Plücker Coordinates Form). The Plücker coordinates give an isomorphism:

$$\bigwedge^2 \mathbb{R}^4 \cong \mathbb{R}^6$$
